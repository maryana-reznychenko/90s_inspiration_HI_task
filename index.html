When you look on the world in another way

"Life is like a box of chocolates, you never know what you're gonna get."

This epigraph serves as a thematic cornerstone for the entire movie, encapsulating the essence of the plot and the character of Forrest Gump. It reflects the idea that life is unpredictable, full of surprises, and often beyond our control. Forrest's journey throughout the film is a testament to this philosophy as he encounters a wide range of unexpected events, people, and situations, all of which shape his remarkable life. The quote also underscores Forrest's simple and optimistic outlook on life, as he approaches each moment with an open heart and a sense of wonder, regardless of the challenges and uncertainties that come his way.

Some facts about the movie

The film was produced in 1993 with the world premiere on the 14th of October 1994 the next year would be the movie's thirtieth anniversary.

The film budget was 55 mln$. Only during the first weekend in the USA and Canada "Forrest Gump" earned 24 mln$.

Forrest Gump won six Academy Awards for Best Picture, Best Actor in a Leading Role, Best Director, Best Visual Effects, Best Adapted Screenplay, and Best Film Editing. Hanks became the first actor since Spencer Tracy to win two consecutive Academy Awards for Best Actor; he won the previous year for Philadelphia.
